Biography: Cristiano Ronaldo

Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro (born February 5, 1985) is a Portuguese professional footballer broadly considered one of the greastest players in history.He captains both Saudi Pro League club AL-Nassr and the Portugal national team, and has scored over 970 official career goals.

Early Life and Beginnings

Born into a humble family in Funchal, Madeira, Ronaldo grew up in poverty alongside three older siblings. His mother, Maria Dolores, worked as a cook and cleaner, and his father, Jose Dinis, was nearly derailed by a racing heart condition requiring surgery, but he made a full recovery to join Sporting Lisbon's youth academy.

The Breakout(2002-2003)

Ronaldo made his debut for Sporting Lisbon in 2002. In a 2003 friendly match against Manchester United, his explosive, dynamic dribbling dazzled the opposing team,prompting manager Sir Alex Ferguson to sign him shortly after for an initial 19 million dollars.

Manchester United(2003-2009)

During his firet stint at Old Trafford, Ronaldo developed into a world-class winger. He helped the club win three successive Premier League titles(2007-2009) and the UEFA Champions League(2008). In 2008, he earned his first Ballon d'OR as the world's best player.

Juventus and AL-Nassr.

Ronaldo joined italian giants Juventus in 2018, winnig two Seria A titles, before returning to Manchester United in 2021. In late 2022, he transferred to Saudi Arabia's AL-Nassr.

International Legacy

Internationally,Ronaldo has represented Portugal in five FIFA World cups and five UEFA European Championships. He also became both the most-capped male player in international football history and all-time top men's international goalscorer.
